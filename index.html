Call of Duty: Warzone Commits to a Massive Weapon Change Ahead of Black Ops 7 Season 1

The Devs at Raven Software are rolling out significant weapon and attatchment-system changes ahead of Black Ops 7 Season 1. Attachment-building is being tightened

and now weapons are limited to maximum of 5 attachments, and support for the old "Gunfire" wildcard is being removed. Meaning load-out choices matter more than ever.

Athe the same time, the "Overkill" benefit is now default. So players don't need to sacrifice a wildcard slot to dual-carry. The overall philosophy driving the update is:

Attachments will have a "clearer trade-offs and defined roles", so guns and builds are more purpose-driven instead of "jack-of-all-trades." This should encourage variety, experimentation,

and more meaningful decisions when customizing


New Dead Rising Game Has Leaks Revealing Settings and Other Features

According to a leak, a new Dead Rising entry, codenamed "Rec", is reportedly in development at Capcom. Productions is theorized to begin sometime in 2023

The game is said to bring back protagonist Frank West and will not be set in a mall, but on a massive enclosed movie-studio lot in Hollywood. The plot is said to

involve a dereanged film director trapping Frank and other survivors in the studio and forcing them to a deadly trials. All while chaos ensues due to a zombie

outbreak. Fans may also get a return of classic franchise elements such as: Frank's camera for pictures, strict time-limit mechanics, and survivor-rescue system.

There is no offical release date or confirmation from Capcom just yet, so it all remains a rumor for now.


Destiny 2: Renegades is Inspired by Star Wars

Renegades is heavily inspired by Star Wars, bringing in sci-fi "Space Western" vibes. New Weapons tupes, Outlaw-styled gameplay, and a darker underworld theme. But developers stress it

remains a "Destiny story at heart." The expansion introduces a new playable mode called Lawless Frontier where players take on this risky, high-reward jobs either solo or in firearms. Players also

get new gear such as energy-blaster weapons, exotic and legendary gear, and new abilities tied to the Renegade theme. Story-wise, Renegades continues the ongoing Fate Saga where players side with a shady

syndicate, defy traditional authorities, and tackle a rising threat.


Pokemon Legends: Z-A Has a Problem With AFK Players

The game includes online multiplayer via Z-A battle club, a mode where up to four players face off in a real-time battle. But many matches have become frustrating

as a lot of players are reportedly refusing to move or interact at all for the whole match, essentially going "AFK" (away-from-keyboard). That behavior ruins the experience

for other players. Matches often fail to progress meaningfully, making online play feel broken. Beacuse this is happening often and apparently without any penalty right now, a significant

portion of the community considers online mulitplayer in Z-A "Unplayable"


Thousands of ARC Raiders Players Petition for a New Item With Big PvP Implications

Over 6,000 players of ARC Raiders have signed a petition requesting that a new usable in-game item- a handcuff- be added. The idea is that instead of killing downed players, you could

cuff them. This can also add a new strategic and role-playing layers to PvP: e.g. capturing players, forcing surrender, or controlling looting/escape. As of now, handcuffs only appear in the

game as "ruined" / loot-recycle items and not as usable mechanic in raids. If implemented, this mechanic could significantly change the dynamic of raids. It could allow players to disable opponets

without outright killing them, potentially giving rise to new tatics, betrayals, or even "mercy"-style escapes.
